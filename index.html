Your current focus: Head lift
Coach-identified swing issue — see Coach Board for homework
  • !
    What head lifting costs you. When your head comes up early, your shoulders follow, the club loses its path, and you typically skull or fat the shot. At a 95-handicap level this alone can cost 4–6 strokes per round.
  • 1
    Fix: "Logo to the ball" drill. Put a coin or tee behind the ball. Keep your eyes on that spot through impact — don't look up until you hear the ball land.
  • 2
    Fix: Stay down, finish tall. Feel like your chest is pointing at the ground at impact. Your head will naturally come up after the ball is long gone — trust it.
  • 3
    Fix: Listen for contact. On the range, close your eyes after setup and listen for the click of solid contact before looking up. This rewires the instinct to peek.

    Ocean Resorts — hole-by-hole strategy
    Course-specific tips for your home track
    Danger holes — water in play
    • Hole 4 (Par 4): No driver — 3-wood or long iron for approach over water to a shallow green surrounded by sand. Club up and aim center of green.
    • Hole 6 (Par 4): Play tee shot at the cluster of trees across the pond. Water pinches in front of the green — take extra club on approach.
    • Hole 9 (Par 5): Avoid hidden pond in front of green. Hit to proper tier of the severe two-tiered green. Bogey is a great score here.
    • Hole 18 (Par 4): Pond sneaks into the left fairway. Play tee shot right side, avoid OB left. Great finishing hole — don't blow up here.
    Scoring opportunities — your birdie holes
    • Hole 1 (Par 4): Play left side to avoid fairway bunkers, short approach shot. Two putts = par. Excellent start.
    • Hole 16 (Par 4): Short dogleg right. Aim tee shot at left greenside bunker and get an easy pitch — great birdie chance.
    • Hole 17 (Par 5): Short par 5 out of the woods. Keep second shot left, avoid fairway bunker. Generous landing areas — go for it in two.
    • Hole 15 (Par 5): Reachable in two but not as long as it looks. Avoid the trees and you have a real eagle/birdie chance.
    General course management
    • Dogleg holes (10, 11, 13): Never cut the corner. Hit to the safe side of the fairway and play the course as designed.
    • Par 3s (3, 8, 12, 14): Hole 3 needs a long straight iron through trees. Hole 8 is long and undulating — aim center. Hole 14 has a tricky 3-level green.
    • Always aim fat part of green. Ocean Resorts has well-bunkered greens — the safe play to the center gives you a longer putt but avoids the big number.
    🧠
    General course IQ
    • T
      Club down for fairways. A 3-wood in the fairway is 2x more valuable than a driver in the rough at your level.
    • A
      Take one more club than you think. Amateurs almost always come up short. The back of the green is still a green.
    • G
      Bogey is always available. From trouble, get back to the fairway first. Don't compound one mistake.
    • B
      Pick 2–3 birdie holes per round. At Ocean Resorts those are 1, 15, 16, 17. Play aggressively only there — survive everywhere else.

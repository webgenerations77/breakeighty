Current focus: Head lift
Coach-identified issue — see Coach Board for homework
  • !
    What head lifting costs you. When your head comes up early, your shoulders follow, the club loses path — skulled or fat shots. This alone costs 4–6 strokes per round.
  • 1
    "Logo to the ball" drill. Put a coin behind the ball. Keep eyes on that spot through impact — don't look up until you hear the ball land.
  • 2
    Stay down, finish tall. Feel your chest pointing at the ground at impact. Head comes up naturally after the ball is gone — trust it.
  • 3
    Listen for contact. On the range, close your eyes and listen for the click of solid contact before looking up. Rewires the "peek" instinct.

    Ocean Resorts — course strategy
    Your home track, hole by hole
    Danger holes — water in play
    • Hole 4 (Par 4): NO DRIVER. 3-wood or long iron. Approach carries water — extra club, aim center.
    • Hole 6 (Par 4): Aim at tree cluster across pond. Water pinches green front — extra club always.
    • Hole 9 (Par 5): Hidden pond front of green. Play correct tier of severe two-tiered green. Bogey = win.
    • Hole 18 (Par 4): Pond sneaks left. OB also left. Play tee shot right side. Finish clean.
    Scoring opportunities
    • Hole 1 (Par 4): Play left, avoid bunkers. Good opener — make par.
    • Hole 16 (Par 4): Short dogleg right. Aim at left greenside bunker — best birdie hole.
    • Hole 17 (Par 5): Short par 5. Let it rip. Keep second shot left. Real birdie chance.
    • Hole 15 (Par 5): Reachable in two. Avoid trees. Eagle/birdie chance!
    • T
      Club down for fairways. 3-wood in the fairway beats driver in the rough every time.
    • A
      Take one more club. Amateurs come up short. Back of the green is still a green.
    • G
      Bogey is always available. From trouble: get back to the fairway first.
    • B
      Pick your birdie holes. Holes 1, 15, 16, 17. Attack only there — survive everywhere else.

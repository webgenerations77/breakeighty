Current focus: Head lift
Coach-identified issue — see Coach Board for homework
  • !
    What head lifting costs you. When your head comes up early, your shoulders follow, the club loses path, and you skull or fat the shot. At a 95-handicap level this costs 4–6 strokes per round.
  • 1
    "Logo to the ball" drill. Put a coin behind the ball. Keep eyes on that spot through impact — don't look up until you hear the ball land.
  • 2
    Stay down, finish tall. Feel like your chest points at the ground at impact. Your head naturally comes up after the ball is gone — trust it.
  • 3
    Listen for contact. On the range, close your eyes after setup and listen for the click of solid contact before looking up. This rewires the "peek" instinct.

    Ocean Resorts — hole-by-hole strategy
    Course-specific tips for your home track
    Danger holes — water in play
    • Hole 4 (Par 4): No driver. 3-wood or long iron. Approach carries water to shallow green — extra club, aim center.
    • Hole 6 (Par 4): Aim tee shot at tree cluster across the pond. Water pinches green front — take extra club on approach.
    • Hole 9 (Par 5): Hidden pond front of green. Play correct tier of the severe two-tiered green. Bogey = win here.
    • Hole 18 (Par 4): Pond sneaks into left fairway. Play tee shot right side. Don't blow up on the closer.
    Scoring opportunities — your birdie holes
    • Hole 1 (Par 4): Play left to avoid bunkers. Short approach. Two putts = great opening par.
    • Hole 16 (Par 4): Short dogleg right. Aim tee shot at left greenside bunker — easy pitch, best birdie hole on the course.
    • Hole 17 (Par 5): Short par 5 — let it rip. Keep second shot left, generous landing areas. Real chance for eagle/birdie.
    • Hole 15 (Par 5): Reachable in two, not as long as it looks. Avoid trees. Eagle/birdie chance!
    General management
    • Dogleg holes (10, 11, 13): Never cut the corner. Hit to the safe side and play as designed.
    • Par 3s (3, 8, 12, 14): Hole 3 needs a straight long iron. Hole 8 undulates — aim center. Hole 14 has a tricky 3-level green.
    • Always aim fat part of green. Ocean Resorts greens are well-bunkered. Safe play to center avoids the big number.
    🧠
    General course IQ
    • T
      Club down for fairways. 3-wood in the fairway is 2× more valuable than driver in the rough at your level.
    • A
      Take one more club than you think. Amateurs almost always come up short. The back of the green is still a green.
    • G
      Bogey is always available. From trouble, get back to fairway first. Don't compound the mistake.
    • B
      Pick 2–3 birdie holes per round. At Ocean Resorts: holes 1, 15, 16, 17. Play aggressively only there — survive everywhere else.
